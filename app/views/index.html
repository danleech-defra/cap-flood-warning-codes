Common Alerting Protocol demo

This site contains mock ups of flood warning messages presented with Common Alerting Protocol (CAP) information as it could be displayed on the new Flood Information Service pages on GOV.UK.

These are just examples. Not all combinations of CAP will be appropriate for the flood warning service.

It is also possible to stipulate "Response" with specific actions for the incident. These should also be considered for inclusion in a CAP based flood warning service to give clear guidance on what action people should take in that specific flood incident.

      Detailed example messages

      Minor flooding

      This is broadly equivalent to the existing Flood Alert status. They should be issued for low impact flooding. This could be shallow flooding of roads and footpaths, or flooding of areas with little to no impact on people - such as fields or farmland.

      Severe flooding

      This is equivalent of Severe Flood Warning status. They should be issued when there is a risk to life or significant disruption for people in that area. Flood waters may be deep or fast flowing. Evacuations may be required and emergency services and local authorities will be heavily involved.

      Extreme flooding

      This is not currently used in the flood warning service. We could use this status for exceptional events, where there is extraordinary threat to life or property. As an example, a failure of a reservoir or defence where rapid destruction of properties behind the assets is expected.
