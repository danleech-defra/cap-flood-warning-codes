Common Alerting Protocol demo

This site contains mock ups of flood warning messages presented with Common Alerting Protocol (CAP) information as it could be displayed on the new Flood Information Service pages on GOV.UK.

The main CAP fields looked at are:

  • Severity
  • Certainty
  • Response

"Urgency" could be used as a mechanism for prioritising the message sending process - for ecample, a warning with an "immediate" urgency will be issued before a warning with a "future" urgency. We could use the "Response" field to show specific actions for the incident.

We do not have an equivalent of the "Extreme" severity status in the flood warning service. We could use this status for exceptional events, where there is extraordinary threat to life or property. As an example, a failure of a reservoir or defence where rapid destruction of properties behind the assets is expected.

These are just examples. Not all combinations of CAP will be appropriate for the flood warning service.

Detailed example messages

Minor flooding

This is broadly equivalent to the existing Flood Alert status. They should be issued for low impact flooding. This could be shallow, slow moving flooding of roads and footpaths, or flooding of areas with little to no impact on people - such as fields or farmland.

Severe flooding

This is equivalent of Severe Flood Warning status. They should be issued when there is a risk to life or significant disruption for people in that area - including infrastructure impacts. Flood waters may be deep or fast flowing. Evacuations may be required and emergency services and local authorities will be involved.
